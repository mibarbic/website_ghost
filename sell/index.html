Waze Desktop Client

5 December 2021 / Projects

Problem Statement

Client is requesting to have web page redesigned with a focus of functionality in mind.

The redesigned site for the client uses a mix of modern UI design concepts to present the end user with a fresh and clean user experience.

Additionally, the site truncates on the previous informational approach and presents a functionality first stance, allowing the end user to be able to use the web-client to the extent that they would with the mobile app and with other competitors.

Color Palette

The color palette used for the re-design web client adopts colors based off of the mobile application’s icons. Use of the analogous color palette allows the application to retain its playful and colorful charisma.

Typography Choices

The typeface used for the redesigned client is Gothic A1. The font was chosen because of its readability, sleekness, and modern look.

Upon choosing Gothic A1 as the typeface, the client’s type and logo was redesigned to reflect the modernity of the application.

New Assets

Assets were not provided by the client, resulting in all used assets to be made from scratch. Cues from the mobile client’s application icons, and principles from Microsoft’s Fluent Design , Apple’s iOS design, and Google’s Material Design 2 were implemented to create a bold, unique, playful, yet functional user experience.

20, San Clemente, CA

Introverted and creative.

An early adopter of technology and existing Waze user.

Not into social media, enjoys photography, traveling outdoor activities.

Wants a navigation app that delivers up to date traffic reports in real time.

Prefers a navigation app that lets the user know if there is a nearby cop.

Has an issues with too much traffic information being presented at once.

Prefers brands such as Apple Inc., Patagonia, and Nikon.

Insights

User will use the web-client:

Most likely to use map feature.

Most likely to check account details.

Most likely to look at police reports ahead of time.

Persona II: Audrey

19, West Covina, CA

Ambiverted and creative.

Very late adopter of technology and not an existing Waze user.

Casually uses social media, enjoys dancing, eating out, and socializing.

Is more likely to download an application that improves her life in someway.

Tends to have short attention span and can be fidgety.

Wants a navigation app that gets her from point A to point B as fast and efficient as possible.

Dislikes navigation applications that give directions last minute.

Prefers brands such as Amazon, Ikea, and Costco.

Insights

User will use the web-client:

Most likely to use map feature.

Most likely to check account details.

Most likely to preview a route ahead of time.

Most likely to look for restaurants.

Site Map

The redesigned Waze site map take the existing Waze web client and pushes a functionality first approach towards the user.

Wireframes

The wire frames served as a rough idea as what the final deliverable would look like.

Upon proceeding with creating the mock-ups, several suggestions from survey groups were taken into consideration.

The redesigned web client puts the Live Map element that was buried in the old web client front and center to promote usage of the site’s functionality while still retaining the pressure on users to download their mobile client.
